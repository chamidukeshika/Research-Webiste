Project Domain

Research framing and implementation path

A compact view of the gap, problem, objectives, methodology, and technology stack.

Research focus

Independent mobility with low-latency edge intelligence

The system aims to deliver practical, real-time awareness without relying on slow or privacy-sensitive cloud workflows.

Core constraints

  • Portability and daily usability
  • Affordable implementation choices
  • Fast response under real-world conditions
  • Multimodal cues instead of single-channel alerts

Survey stream 01

Smart mobility and obstacle avoidance

Purely ultrasonic solutions struggle in cluttered or noisy environments. Hybrid studies show better navigation reliability when sensing and visual understanding are fused into the same workflow.

Survey stream 02

Edge AI for assistive devices

Research is moving away from cloud-heavy inference toward local execution, enabling lower latency, stronger privacy, and more dependable field usage.

Survey stream 03

Adaptive feedback design

Multimodal warnings such as voice and vibration improve reaction quality over single-channel systems, especially when urgency and direction are prioritized.

Research gap

Existing solutions optimize isolated functions rather than the full assistive loop

Many smart stick concepts improve one dimension at a time, such as distance detection, visual recognition, or user feedback. Fewer solutions demonstrate a practical, unified architecture that remains lightweight, cost-aware, and fully executable on edge hardware.

The workspace structure also highlights the academic requirement for staged evidence, reproducible progress, and documentation readiness across every review.

Research problem

How can a smart assistive stick deliver dependable environmental awareness in real time?

Need

Safer movement support for visually impaired users during everyday mobility.

Constraint

Hardware must stay practical, portable, and responsive without cloud dependence.

Challenge

Perception, decision logic, and feedback must work together instead of as separate features.

Research objectives

Five objectives structure the implementation path

  • Design a multimodal perception stack using distance sensing and visual features.
  • Implement a low-latency edge inference workflow for near real-time warnings.
  • Develop context-aware audio feedback aligned with urgency and user movement.
  • Evaluate performance through accuracy, response-time, and usability analysis.
  • Document the work across proposal, progress, checklist, and final assessment stages.

Methodology

Implementation is sequenced as a staged research pipeline

Phase 01

Literature review, requirement definition, and architecture planning.

Phase 02

Prototype build for sensing, detection, and feedback modules.

Phase 03

Edge-model tuning, benchmark measurement, and subsystem integration.

Phase 04

User-oriented evaluation, refinement, and final academic packaging.

Technology stack

Core technical ingredients behind the concept

Edge AI inference Computer vision Ultrasonic sensors Sensor fusion Embedded C and C++ Python prototyping Model quantization Audio alert pipeline
